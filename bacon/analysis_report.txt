3. FRAUD PATTERN DETECTION RESULTS (Q5, Q8, Q9, Q10, Q11)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

All 8 embedded fraud patterns were successfully detected:

Pattern 1 — Structuring/Smurfing:
  STR-001 detected 218 alerts, ALL on known fraud accounts (100% precision).
  Q5 identified accounts with 3+ sub-threshold deposits in a single month,
  with totals ranging from $27,000–$250,000 (clearly above the CTR threshold
  when aggregated). Avg deposit in fraud cluster: $9,456.

Pattern 2 — Rapid Velocity:
  VEL-001 detected 192 alerts with 100% precision. Maximum observed velocity:
  49 transactions in a 1-hour window (vs. normal max of ~5).
  Q11 shows fraud accounts have avg_vel_1h = 8.2 vs 0.07 for normal accounts
  — a 117x multiplier, making this the highest-signal rule.

Pattern 3 — Round-Trip Transfers:
  Q9 (self-join analysis) identified 58 wire-out/wire-in pairs to the same
  counterparty within 48 hours. Amounts closely match (avg difference < 3%),
  consistent with fee deductions in layering schemes.

Pattern 4 — Geographic Risk:
  GEO-001 (sanctioned countries) fired 9 times with 100% precision and highest
  average score (61.4). Counterparties in Iran, North Korea, Russia, and Cuba
  detected. GEO-002 (very high risk) added 24 more alerts.

Pattern 5 — Layering:
  The A->B->C->D chain pattern was captured by a combination of VEL rules,
  GEO-003, and BEH-003 (round-amount large wires). Wire transfers showed
  systematic 2-5% deductions per hop — classic layering signature.

Pattern 6 — Dormant Activation:
  Q10 found 15+ accounts with gaps >= 180 days followed by sudden large
  transactions. Reactivation amounts ranged $10k–$50k, significantly above
  the account's historical average (z-score > 4 in Q8).

Pattern 7 — Unusual Amounts:
  BEH-001 flagged 148 individual customers with transactions > $50k USD.
  Q8 z-score analysis found 2,720 transactions with z > 4, with fraud accounts
  accounting for a disproportionate share (typical z-score > 8 for P7 fraud).

Pattern 8 — Cross-Account Smurfing:
  STR-003 detected only 6 alerts (high-precision condition), but the broader
  STR-002 and cross-account velocity analysis in Q5 captured the full picture
  of coordinated multi-account sub-threshold deposits.

4. RULE ENGINE PERFORMANCE (Q4, Q19, Q23)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

High-precision rules (>= 90%):
  STR-001 Sub-threshold cash deposit:   100% precision, 218 alerts
  STR-003 Cross-account smurfing:        100% precision, 6 alerts
  VEL-001 Rapid transaction burst:       100% precision, 192 alerts
  GEO-001 Sanctioned country:            100% precision, 9 alerts
  GEO-002 Very high risk country:        100% precision, 24 alerts
  BEH-001 Unusual large amount:           91.9% precision, 148 alerts

Lower-precision rules (broad, generate leads):
  GEO-003 High-risk country new cp:       7% precision, 17,813 alerts
  VEL-003 24h volume spike:               8% precision, 10,493 alerts
  BEH-002 Dormant activation:             8.2% precision, 5,947 alerts

The low-precision rules are intentional: they cast a wide net for investigation.
When combined with the composite scoring model, high-noise rules like GEO-003
still produce value — they elevate scores when combined with other risk factors.

Score calibration:
  Rule co-occurrence analysis (Q15) shows that GEO-003 + VEL-003 + THR-002
  frequently fire together on the same transaction, producing compound scores
  of 50-70. These multi-rule alerts warrant priority attention.

False positive concentration (Q23):
  Low-risk, verified, non-PEP customers generate the majority of alerts from
  broad rules. These are prime tuning targets: raising thresholds or adding
  customer-type filters to VEL-003 and BEH-002 could reduce noise by ~40%
  while retaining >95% of true positives.

10. RECOMMENDATIONS FOR PRODUCTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. TUNE BEH-002 (Dormant Activation): Add minimum account age check and
   require velocity_7d = 0 for 2+ consecutive weeks, not just the current week.
   This would reduce 5,947 → ~500 alerts while retaining true dormancy signals.

2. ADD AGGREGATE STRUCTURING RULE: A 7-day rolling window rule that flags
   customers with >$25,000 in cash deposits across any rolling week would
   catch more P1/P8 fraud without the noise of STR-002's 24h window.

3. IMPLEMENT ALERT SCORING DECAY: High-scoring alerts on consistently
   "normal" customers should decay over time. PEP high-value alerts should
   not repeatedly fire on the same recurring payment.

4. NETWORK GRAPH INTEGRATION: The shared counterparty clusters (Q20) warrant
   a proper graph database integration (e.g., Neo4j) for community detection
   at scale.

5. REAL-TIME VELOCITY: Move velocity calculations from batch ETL to a
   streaming system (Kafka + Redis sorted sets) for real-time threshold alerts
   during high-velocity fraud bursts.
